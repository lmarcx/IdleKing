# BOSS DATABASE — IdleKing

## Purpose

This document defines the boss philosophy, taxonomy, progression role, reward structure, replayability framework, and MVP boss roster for IdleKing.

This is a V1 design source of truth.

Detailed loot tables may be refined later after:

# 1. Boss Design Pillars

Bosses are a core pillar of IdleKing.

They serve multiple purposes:

- narrative progression
- gameplay mastery checks
- chapter climax encounters
- economy progression
- crafting progression
- world progression gating
- replayable challenge content

Bosses are not only story encounters.

They are long-term repeatable content through alternate modes.

---

# 2. Boss Taxonomy

## 2.1 Story Boss

Major canonical bosses tied directly to narrative progression.

Characteristics:
- story cutscenes
- multi-phase encounters
- chapter progression
- first-clear rewards
- narrative consequences
- era unlock progression

Examples:
- Amalgame des Ténèbres
- Ombre du Dragon
- Allaeva
- Seigneur de la Pluie Corrompu
- Waqt

---

## 2.2 Mid Boss / Dungeon Boss

Intermediate bosses.

Purpose:
- mechanic teaching
- difficulty ramp
- dungeon gating
- crafting material generation

Characteristics:
- fewer cinematic sequences
- 1–2 phases
- lower narrative impact than Story Bosses

Examples:
- Seigneur de la Pluie Déchu
- Archimage

---

## 2.3 Optional Boss

Non-mandatory encounters.

Purpose:
- challenge
- rare loot
- hidden content
- completion rewards

Characteristics:
- optional access
- replayable
- can include secret narrative reveals

---

## 2.4 Repeatable Boss

Bosses accessible after story completion through alternate gameplay modes.

Access:
- Kaléidoscope
- World Gate
- challenge modes
- future alternate game modes

Narrative rule:
Canonical story deaths remain final.

Replay encounters represent:
- echoes
- temporal recreations
- challenge manifestations
- alternate mode simulations

All boss categories may have repeatable versions.

---

# 3. Boss Reward Philosophy

## 3.1 Guaranteed Rewards

Standard rewards may include:

- XP
- ECU
- Generic Boss Tokens
- baseline crafting materials

---

## 3.2 First Clear Rewards

Story progression rewards.

Possible rewards:
- large XP
- World XP
- ECU
- Generic Boss Tokens
- named crafting materials
- direct gear chance
- chapter progression items
- era unlock catalysts

Only end-of-chapter major bosses grant era progression items.

---

## 3.3 Replay Rewards

Replay rewards are intentionally reduced.

Replay rewards:
- reduced XP
- reduced ECU
- NO World XP
- Generic Boss Tokens
- named material chance
- gear drop chance
- baseline materials

This prevents progression exploitation.

---

# 4. Loot Philosophy

## 4.1 Boss Tokens

MVP uses:

Generic Boss Token

Future uses:
- exchange vendors
- cosmetics
- challenge progression
- exclusive crafting sinks
- special upgrade systems

---

## 4.2 Named Drops

Bosses may drop identity-linked crafting materials.

Examples:
- Dragon Ember Core
- Frozen Queen Tear
- Flood Sovereign Fragment

Named drops are primarily crafting materials.

Story-specific narrative relics are separate systems.

---

## 4.3 Direct Equipment Drops

Bosses may directly drop equipment.

Philosophy:
semi-unique themed generated gear

This means:
- generated stat rolls
- themed item pools
- boss identity flavor
- non fully fixed handcrafted items (for MVP)

---

## 4.4 Rarity Scope

Current rarity ladder:

- Common
- Uncommon
- Rare
- Epic
- Legendary
- Mythic
- Divine
- Ancient

MVP scope:

Boss drops capped at:
Common → Legendary

Mythic / Divine / Ancient reserved for later chapters.

---

# 5. Boss Encounter Philosophy

## 5.1 Multi Phase Rules

Story Bosses:
YES

Mid Bosses:
optional

Recommended MVP:
- Amalgame des Ténèbres: 1 phase
- Seigneur de la Pluie Déchu: 1–2 phases
- Ombre du Dragon: 2 phases
- Amalgame du Givre: 1 phase
- Archimage: 2 phases
- Allaeva: 2 phases

---

## 5.2 Cinematics

Major Story Bosses only:
- intro cinematic
- phase transition cinematic
- death cinematic

Mid Bosses:
minimal or no cinematics

---

## 5.3 Checkpoints

Boss checkpoints are placed before encounters.

Dungeon progress and accumulated rewards are checkpoint-saved.

On player death:
- retry from checkpoint
- no frustration-driven resource wipe

IdleKing is not intended to use Souls-style punitive corpse recovery.

---

## 5.4 Enrage Mechanics

MVP:
NO enrage systems

May be added later in challenge modes.

---

# 6. Boss Families

## 6.1 Amalgames

Recurring boss family.

Definition:

Amalgames are unstable hybrid entities born from:
- corruption
- creation
- destruction

Characteristics:
- larger monster-type bosses
- unstable forms
- destructive behavior
- identity-linked thematic variants

Known examples:
- Amalgame des Ténèbres
- Amalgame du Givre

Potential future:
- Amalgame du Déluge
- Amalgame de Chair
- Amalgame du Chaos

---

## 6.2 Humanoid Duel Bosses

Primary boss archetype.

Focus:
- duel combat
- readable patterns
- mechanical mastery
- narrative emotional impact

Examples:
- Archimage
- Allaeva
- Noah Corrompu
- Waqt

---

## 6.3 Spectacle Bosses

Large cinematic encounters.

Examples:
- Ombre du Dragon
- True Dragon
- late-game catastrophes
